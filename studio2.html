  CNET : Electronics : Top 10 must-haves  


(Updated 11/5/01)

Any technojunkie worth his or her salt salivates at the mere thought of having the latest and greatest gizmos. After all, it's all about who has bragging rights of owning the hottest toys. So whether you're a gadget newbie or a hardened technophile, there's sure to be something that you'll love in our picks for the 10 best gadgets so far in 2001.



Check latest prices 		 1.  Samsung SPH-I300
Samsung successfully melds a wireless phone and Palm OS PDA into a single, sleek device with the SPH-I300. You can surf the Web, send and receive wireless e-mail, and run all your favorite Palm applications on its bright color screen. It's also loaded with all the features of a cell phone, including vibrate mode, multiple ring tones, and call history. If you don't want to carry both a phone and a PDA, check out this gadget.  
More wireless phones
 
 

Check latest prices 		 2.  Sony DCR-PC9 
Weighing just 17.2 ounces, Sony's DCR-PC9 DV camcorder is hardly noticeable when stowed away in your luggage. While it may not offer quite the image quality of Sony's upper-echelon camcorders, it still delivers sharp images and is suitable for documenting vacations or guerilla filmmaking. This is currently our favorite compact camcorder.  
More camcorders
 
 

Check latest prices 		 3.  Panasonic DVD-RP91 
Granted, this one's for folks who have high-end, HD-ready TVs, but it's one of the best progressive-scan DVD players we've reviewed and, thankfully, not the most expensive. It delivers superb, razor-sharp video with colors that really pop. On top of that, it supports DVD-Audio discs for your listening pleasure. 
More DVD players
 
 

Check latest prices 		 4.  Archos Jukebox HD-MP3 Recorder 
A hard-drive-based MP3 player, the Archos Jukebox stores an unbelievable 100 hours' worth of MP3 music and is still light enough to be portable. It's easy to save music (or any other type of data you want) from your PC or Mac to its 6GB hard drive. Its combination of storage capabilities and lightweight, compact design have made it a big hit with our editors. 
More MP3 players
 
 

Check latest prices 		 5.  Toshiba SD-P1500 
Looking for a way to play your DVDs on the go without lugging around a laptop? This supercompact portable DVD has a sharp 7-inch display and good audio support. So whether you're looking to play your own in-flight movies or want a DVD player to plug into a TV wherever you land, this is the one electronics carry-on required for your next trip.  
More DVD players
 
 

Check latest prices 		 6.  Sony CLIE PEG-N760C
Sony has outdone every other Palm OS handheld maker with its top-of-the-line CLIE. Its high-resolution, reflective color screen is by far the best. And it's the only Palm device with built-in audio support so that you can listen to MP3s and watch videos. You'll also get a hefty bundle of extra software to show off all those high-tech features.  
More handhelds
 
 

Check latest prices  		 7.  Canon PowerShot G2 
Canon delivers a powerful 4-megapixel digital camera for serious photographers. The G2 bristles with buttons and dials so that you can quickly access its myriad controls without digging through menus. Plus, its clever, flip-out LCD makes it easy to shoot extreme high- and low-angle shots.  
More digital cameras
 
 

Check latest prices  		 8.  Bantam Interactive Explor BA350 
This tiny, digital music player packs 128MB of built-in RAM, a MultiMedia Card slot for adding more memory, and a rechargeable battery into a package not that much bigger than a cigarette lighter. Better yet, it costs only $200, and Bantam throws in seven colored faceplates and a nifty protective carrying case. Needless to say, we're impressed. 
More MP3 players
 
 

Check latest prices 		 9.  Sony DVP-F21
This may not be the best-performing or feature-rich DVD player on the planet, but it sure is stylish. Hang it on your wall, stand it up on a shelf, or lay it flat, the DVP-F21 is a fashion statement wherever it's placed. While it's not a match for high-end home theaters, it delivers respectable video quality and does a good job of playing scratched-up discs.  
More DVD players
 
 

Check latest prices  		 10.  Game Boy Advance 
Imagine being able to carry around the power of an old Super Nintendo in the palm of your hand. This small, sturdy, expandable time killer is perfect for helping ease those long commutes. With a promising collection of addictive games at a reasonable price, you can't go wrong here. Better still is the ability to plug it into Nintendo's upcoming GameCube for extra functionality.  
More video game machines
 
 

Check latest prices 		 The next great gadget:   Apple iPod 
Apple has been known to come up with some slickly designed products, but it's really outdone itself with the iPod, a soon to be released portable MP3 player. With its elegant white face, big LCD, smooth navigation ring that takes you through all your tunes (the device offers 5GB worth of storage), and superfast FireWire connection, it was love at first sight for our editors. So what if iPod costs $399? And so what if it works only with Macs running the latest versions of OS 9 and OS X? You're gonna want one of these things when it goes on sale November 10.
